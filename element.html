The International Cricket Council (ICC) is the global governing body of cricket. It was founded as the Imperial Cricket Conference in 1909 by representatives from England, and South Africa. In 1965<< /em>, the body was renamed as the International Cricket Conference and adopted its current name in 1987. ICC has its headquarters in Dubai, United Arab Emirates.
The ICC currently has 110 member nations : 12 full members that play Test matches, and 98 associate members
